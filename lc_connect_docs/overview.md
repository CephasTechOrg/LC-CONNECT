# LC Connect — Overview

## 1. Product Vision

**LC Connect** is a mobile-first campus connection app for Livingstone College students. The goal is to help students meet each other naturally, especially when they want friendship, study partners, language exchange, campus activities, or respectful open connections but feel shy or unsure how to start.

The app should make it easier for students to cross social boundaries without pressure, awkwardness, or fear of rejection.

## 2. Core Problem

Many students on campus may want to connect with others but struggle because of:

- Shyness or fear of rejection
- Cultural differences
- International/domestic student separation
- Language barriers
- Not knowing who shares similar interests
- Not knowing who is open to friendship, studying, events, or dating/open connection
- Lack of a simple campus-only platform for safe student discovery

Students may see each other often but still never talk because there is no natural bridge.

## 3. Core Solution

Build a verified student-only mobile app where students can:

- Create a campus profile
- Select what they are looking for
- Discover other students through interactive cards
- Match based on interests, major, language, and intent
- Request mutual connections
- Message only after a mutual connection
- Join or create campus activities
- Find study partners and language exchange partners
- Block/report users for safety

## 4. Product Positioning

The app should be positioned as:

> A safe campus connection app for Livingstone College students to find friends, study partners, language exchange partners, activities, and meaningful connections.

It should **not** be marketed mainly as a dating app. Dating/open connection can exist as one optional category, but the main brand should be campus community, friendship, study, and activities.

## 5. MVP Goal

The MVP goal is to test whether students actually want to use the app to connect with each other.

The first version should focus on only the most important features:

1. Student signup/login
2. Profile setup
3. Looking-for preferences
4. Student discovery cards
5. Mutual connection requests
6. Basic messaging
7. Activity board
8. Study partner discovery
9. Language exchange discovery
10. Report/block system

## 6. Target Users

### Primary users

- Livingstone College students
- International students
- Domestic students
- First-year students
- Transfer students
- Students looking for study partners
- Students looking for campus activities

### Secondary users

- Student clubs and organizations
- Resident assistants
- Campus event organizers
- Student affairs/admin team, if the app becomes officially supported later

## 7. Key User Needs

Students need a way to:

- Meet people without awkwardly approaching them in person
- Know who is open to connecting
- Find people with similar interests
- Find students in the same major/classes
- Find safe group activities
- Practice languages
- Find study partners
- Make campus life feel less lonely

## 8. MVP Experience

A student should be able to open the app and quickly understand:

- Who they might connect with
- Why the match is relevant
- What activities are happening
- Who wants to study
- Who wants language exchange
- Which connections are mutual
- Where their messages are

The experience should feel interactive, friendly, and simple.

## 9. Main Navigation Tabs

The MVP mobile app should use five main tabs:

1. **Home** — recommended people and activities
2. **Connect** — student discovery cards
3. **Activities** — campus events and meetups
4. **Messages** — conversations after mutual connection
5. **Profile** — personal profile and settings

## 10. MVP Success Metrics

Track simple metrics to know whether the idea is working:

- Number of student signups
- Profile completion rate
- Number of connection requests sent
- Mutual match rate
- Messages sent after match
- Activities created
- Activities joined
- Reports/blocks per active user
- Weekly active users
- Returning users after 7 days

## 11. Non-Goals for Version 1

Do not build these in the first version:

- Complex AI matching
- Full social media feed
- Stories/reels
- Public comment sections
- Large group chat system
- Payment features
- Advanced admin analytics
- Dating-first experience
- Complex notification engine
- University-wide official integration

These can come later after the MVP proves students actually want the product.
